Kakamega stampede: DPP says no one to blame for school incident

June 15, 2024
Post

- The DPP said even after investigating the stampede, witnesses could not positively identify boys who allegedly blocked the stairway causing the accident

- Haji directed the case to be presented before the magistrate for formal closure by way of a public inquest

- At least 15 pupils lost their lives and dozens injured during the stampede that happened on the evening of Monday, February 3, 2020

- Parents who lost their children in the incident said they were not satisfied with the report and threatened to proceed to court to sue the Ministry of Education

Director of Public Prosecution (DPP) Nordin Haji has said an inquiry into the stampede at Kakamega Primary School has not found anyone to blame for the incident.

Following his investigation, the DPP said there was no "clear criminal act" and "no person had been mentioned" in the incident that left at least 15 pupils dead and scores injured.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported the February 3, 2020, incident happened at around 5pm when pupils at the three-storey school were descending stairs before they could head home.

"I find that there is no clear criminal act that has been committed that is evident. No one person or persons have been mentioned in the incident that saw children lose their lives," a report signed by prosecution counsel, Eric Mutua, read.

According to preliminary reports shortly after the incident, a section of pupils claimed some boys blocked the stairways and this caused other learners to trample upon each other as they rushed home.

However, the DPP stated even after interviewing 16 teachers and 43 pupils, the said boys who allegedly caused the accident could not be identified.

"During or before the stampede, unidentified students (boys) whose numbers vary from between two to four were seen blocking the stairway. Non of the witnesses identified these boys either by name, physical description or which classroom they belong to," the report read in part.

Claims of superstition had also come up during the probe as section of witnesses claimed they saw a mysterious person with a head resembling that of a cow that scared pupils.

Others, for instance, former area senator Boni Khalwale blamed the accident on a church that was conducting its services in one of the classrooms.

The DPP directed the matter to be presented before the magistrate for formal closure.

"I, therefore, direct that the matter be placed before the magistrate under Section 386 of the Criminal Procedure Code for formal closure by way of public inquest," the DPP said.

A section of parents has, however, threatened to sue the Ministry of Education and the school for the incident.

"It is the responsibility of the Ministry of Education to protect children while at school the same way it is my duty to guard my kids once they are at home. We will pursue other means to get justice," said Ibrahim Kiberenge, a parent who lost a child in the incident.
