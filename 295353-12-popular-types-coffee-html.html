12 most popular types of coffee

July 09, 2024
Post

Most people begin and end their day with a hot cup of coffee. People love coffee because of different reasons. For example, it helps to stimulate the brain, calm the nerves, and is a great conversation starter. If you have lots of work or you want to stay up all night, coffee can keep you awake. Furthermore, the stimulating effects of coffee can keep you relaxed throughout the day. But what types of coffee do people drink on a daily basis?

Coffee drinks are based on either coffee or espresso in different strengths of caffein. Some drinks contain sweeteners, flavorings, or alcoholic liquers. However, the most common additions are steamed, formed, or plant based milk.

What are the different types of coffee?

1. Espresso

Espresso is a concentrated and fully-flavored form of coffee served in small “shots.” Unlike other types of coffees, the espresso has a froth known as crema. It is reddish-brown and comes into shape when tiny air bubbles mix with soluble oils of finely-ground and most popular coffee beans. The crema contributes to the alluring aroma and rich flavor of espressos. Having crema on an espresso means it is well made with high-quality and perfectly ground coffee.

How to prepare espresso shot

  • Grind fresh whole coffee beans before brewing. The grind should have the same texture as granulated sugar.
  • Measure the right dose. You need to have the right amount of grind that you will use to fill the portafilter to start preparing the espresso shot.
  • Tamp the coffee for uniformity. Uniformity helps to ensure there is consistent and equal water contact when forced through the grind.
  • Brew the coffee by placing the portafilter into the espresso machine’s brew head. You should then brew it for another 30 seconds.

If it had a fine grind, the right dose, and has been tamped properly, your coffee should turn into a dark color before changing to a golden brown or foamy mixture.

2. Cappuccino

Cappuccino is a coffee drink made of steamed milk and espresso. The espresso-based drink has a recipe that allows for equal ratios of steamed milk, espresso, and frothed milk. In other words, the constituents of the coffee should be a third (1/3) each. Typically, a Cappuccino does not have sweetener or flavored syrup. But you can add them for your taste.

How to prepare a cappuccino

  • Steam your milk and let it simmer until you see bubbles forming around the edges. Do not boil the milk.
  • Use an electric mixer to whip the steamed milk. Increase your mixing speed until it starts to thicken or you get your preferred volume of foam.
  • Brew the coffee using a French press or an automatic drip coffeemaker.
  • Make your cappuccino by mixing a third of espresso, steamed milk, and foam.

If you want your cappuccino to look rich and velvety, use whole milk. You should not use skim milk to make the foam because it will look light and dissolve quickly.

3. Latte

A latte is a type of coffee drink that consists of scolded or steamed milk and espresso (a single shot of coffee). Most people confuse a latte and a cappuccino. Well, lattes are similar to cappuccinos. The difference is that lattes have more steamed milk and less foamed milk. To make a perfect latte, you need one shot of espresso and at least six ounces of steamed milk.

How to prepare a latte

  • Prepare your espresso shot and put it in a latte cup.
  • Pour two-thirds of your steamed milk into the latte cup. The steamed milk should retain some microbubbles.
  • Top your latte using the remaining microbubbles. Spoon them to your latte if possible.

Always use whole milk when preparing a latte. You can add sweeteners or other Coffee flavors to your latte for taste.

4. Caffe macchiato

A Caffe macchiato is a double or single espresso and topped with some dollop of heated and foamed milk. Unlike many other types of coffee, the Caffe macchiato has flavor. It is a strong coffee drink with some sweetness. Whereas most people drink an espresso or cappuccino in the morning, a Caffe macchiato is excellent for an afternoon treat. Caffe macchiato does not taste milky and is not too light.

How to make Caffe macchiato

  • Froth your milk before making caffe macchiato. Your milk should be velvety and creamy
  • Pour the milk in a glass and ensure you have some foam on top.
  • Brew your shots of espresso.
  • Pour the milk into the brewed espresso shot.

You should allow the milk to sit for some few seconds to separate from a dollop of foam.

5. Long Black

The long black is a double or single shot espresso poured over a glass of hot water. Pouring the espresso over hot water helps in preserving the crema. Based on the preparation, you can get a balanced drink that will accentuate the attributes and nuances of the blend of coffee you used. You need a high-quality espresso to achieve this.

How to prepare long black

  • Pour hot water in a glass. You can use between 90 and 120 ml of water.
  • Prepare a double espresso shot.
  • Pour the double espresso shot directly into the hot glass of water.
  • Serve while still hot

Note that the amount of water you use to prepare long black will determine the strength of your coffee.

6. Flat White

Flat white is a type of coffee drink that consists of double or single espresso with steamed milk (microfoam) and fine bubbles. The microfoam milk helps to create a glossy texture. Preparing flat white coffee takes skill. It must have the right flavor otherwise the drink will be blunt.

How to prepare flat white

  • You will need a shot of espresso and shots foamed milk.
  • Pull the shot of espresso and froth the milk.
  • As the milk continues to froth, fold the microbubbles using a spoon to create a smoother and velvety texture.
  • Combine the shot of espresso and shots foamed milk.

You should have a flat white drink with about a quarter inch of the steamed milk on top.

7. Mochaccino

A mochaccino or caffe mocha is a type of cappuccino coffee that contains some chocolate flavoring or syrup. It is based on a shot of espresso and hot milk with some added chocolate sweetener and flavoring. Most varieties of the drink use chocolate syrup and others can have milk or dark chocolate.

How to prepare a mochaccino

  • Brew your shot of espresso.
  • Add a teaspoon of cocoa powder or hot chocolate. Mix it with the espresso.
  • Texture and foam the required amount of milk. Ensure your foam is excellent in quality.
  • Add milk into the mug that contains the shot of chocolate espresso. Top it with some whipped cream.
  • Sprinkle some cocoa powder.

Before you start foaming, you should add cocoa powder to the milk. This will give you a more chocolate-filled experience.

8. Ristretto

A ristretto is a double or single shot espresso with equal amounts of coffee and half amount of water. It is essentially a thicker, more concentrated, and darker espresso extraction. You need a finer grind to make a ristretto to allow you to control the pour speed of the extraction.

How to prepare a ristretto

  • Put your fine grind into the ristretto machine.
  • Tamp the grind as usual.
  • Begin your extraction.
  • Allow the extraction to run until you get 30-40mls of coffee for a double ristretto.

Timing is crucial when making the extraction. For 30mls of the liquid, it should come out of the machine in 30 seconds.

9. Piccolo Latte

A piccolo latte is a type of coffee drink that consists of a double or single espresso mixed with an equal amount of milk. The milk used, in this case, is warm to reduce acidity. Unlike many other coffee drinks, the milk in a piccolo late is steamed, but not texturized or frothy.

How to make piccolo latte

  • Start by preparing your shot of espresso.
  • Steam your milk along with a slight froth or foam texture.
  • Pour the milk into your espresso shot and taste it before serving.

Always use freshly roasted coffee beans to make a piccolo latte.

10.Café au Lait

Café au Lait is a French type of coffee that contains added hot milk. The coffee is different from white coffee. White coffee has cold milk or a whitener. You can create this drink using steamed milk and brewed coffee. Unlike other types of coffees such as espressos, Café au Lait does not have foam on top. But it is possible to add some foam to improve its taste.

How to prepare Café au Lait

You need equal parts of strong hot coffee and steamed milk to make Café au Lait.

  • Gather all your ingredients together into a large cup.
  • Pour the equal parts of strong hot coffee and steamed milk.
  • Reserve the foam and stir the mixture.
  • Spoon the foam on top of the mixture and serve hot.

The taste of your Café au Lait should be less intense. If it is, then you did not mix equal parts of strong hot coffee and steamed milk.

Although these are the ten most popular types of coffee, which type of coffee is the best? Well, it depends on individual tastes. Besides, every region has its coffee style. The differences in style make it difficult to determine which coffee type is the best.

Also, some people prefer taking coffee with a smooth taste and varying acidity levels. Others prefer coffee with a strong taste. But in most cases, the best types of coffee will depend on the coffee beans used to make the drink.
