It was all bliss at the Ol Pejeta Conservancy on Sunday, July 11 when an iconic rhino marked its 32nd birthday.

Identified as Najin, the rhino attained 32 years, the caregivers hailing her for her reserved nature that leaves whoever visits inspired.

"Since her arrival, she has captivated and inspired everyone from her dedicated caregivers to thousands of international visitors and she has shown us exactly what we are fighting to save," said the conservancy in a Facebook post.

Najin began pitching camp at the conservation in 2009 when it came from the Czech Republic alongside her daughter Fatu and their kins namely Suni and Sudan.

However, Suni, a male, died in 2014 at the Ol Pejeta Conservancy, naturals being pointed at as the cause of the death.

Sudan also died in 2018.

Najin and her daughter Fatu are the last two northern white rhinos left in the world, the conservancy working out different projects including the BioRescue to save the species from the brink of extinction.

They are protected round-the-clock by armed guards.

The Ol Pejeta Conservancy

The Conservancy is situated in Laikipia county, west of Nanyuki and between the foothills of the Aberdares and Mount Kenya.

The conservancy works to conserve wildlife, provide a sanctuary for great apes and to generate income through wildlife tourism and complementary enterprises.

Ol Pejeta boasts the largest black rhino sanctuary in East Africa, and in 2013 reached a population milestone of 100 black rhino.

The Sweetwaters Chimpanzee Sanctuary is also found at the conservancy providing a haven for orphaned, abandoned and rescued chimpanzees.
