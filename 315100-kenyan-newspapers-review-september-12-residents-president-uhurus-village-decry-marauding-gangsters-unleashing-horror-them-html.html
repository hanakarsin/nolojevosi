Kenyan newspapers review for September 12: Residents in President Uhuru's village decry marauding ga

February 12, 2024
Post

Even as McDonald Mariga appeals against a decision by the IEBC to stop him from running in the Kibra by-election race, newspapers on Thursday, September 12 reported on Deputy President William Ruto's silence over the aspirant's woes.

The dailies also brought into sharp focus a duel of words between Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru and Third Way Alliance Party leader Ekuru Aukot over fate of Punguza Mizigo Bill.

1. The Standard

Residents of Ichaweri and Ng'enda villages in Gatundu South have asked security agencies to move with speed and bring to book a gang that has been unleashing horror to them.

Ichaweri village hosts the rural home of President Uhuru Kenyatta.

The locals accused police of laxity and not responding promptly to alarm; a development that has led to loss of property, injury and sometimes death.

On Tuesday, the gang struck few metres away from the highly guarded president's rural home.

The attack have since left one person dead, another with a gunshot wound while scores are nursing trauma.

"We are following crucial leads to apprehend members of the gang. We have also intensified patrols to beef up security," said the Gatundu South deputy county commissioner Merisho Maina.

2. Taifa Leo

The Swahili daily has questioned silence of DP Ruto over a spate of woes facing Mariga in the race for Kibra mini poll.

The DP had earlier flaunted the footballer's candidature and even hinted on going to Kibra constituency to lead in campaigning for the Jubilee flag bearer.

It is believed that Mariga's presence in the race that seeks to find Ken Okoth's successor was highly influenced by the deputy president and him remaining tight-lipped on the topic could mean the footballer has been left to swim in the deep end all by himself.

On his part the aspirant hopes the appeal will sail through and the poll agency will give a nod to his nomination paving way for him to be on the ballot.

3. The Star

Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka has said that his party is still consulting on which political outfit it will support in the Kibra by-election.

The Kalonzo led party is still undecided on whom to support in the race slated for November 7 even as speculation was rife that musician Prezzo was among those interested in the seat on a wiper ticket.

With both Musalia Mudavadi led ANC and Moses Wetang'ula's Ford Kenya fielding candidates, Wiper may have a tough decision to choose on whom to support.

The two parties supported Wiper in the Embakasi South by-election that saw Julius Mawathe emerge winner.

"Consultations are still ongoing and we have our supporters on ground who will tell us the candidate we should support,"Kalonzo said.

4. Daily Nation

Kirinyaga Governor Waiguru has vowed not to relent in pushing for a government that will entrench exclusivity and end the system of winner takes it all.

Waiguru also took the chance to slam Aukot's Punguza Mizigo Bill urging Kenyans to reject it claiming it was biased against women and seeking a reduction of Parliamentary seats.

According to the county boss, the country should have three more political positions apart from president and his deputy to foster exclusivity.

"I do not advocate for the government whereby the winner takes it all. When we have more seats in government, Kenyans will feel well represented and there will be no killings after the elections. The BBI is fighting for constitutional change so that we can create more seats in government and it should be backed by all Kenyans," stated Waiguru.
