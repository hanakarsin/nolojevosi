Harmonize's new video Uno deleted from Youtube after Kenyan producer's complaint

May 08, 2024
Post

- Magix Enga had given singer Harmonize a week's notice to take down his new song claiming the Tanzanian singer had stolen his beats

- Uno had already received impressive feedback online and even the star held a promotional tour of the single across Africa

- The beats claimed by Magix are from the Dundaing hit featuring the likes of King Kaka

Uno music video by Tanzanian singer Harmonize has been pulled down from Youtube.

Kenyan producer Magix Enga had sent out a one-week ultimatum to the Bongo Flava star to delete the song online.

Well, it seems Magix was not playing as on Wednesday, November 20, the song was removed from Youtube over copyright infringement.

“One week is over and the song Uno is no longer on Youtube, do not sample Magix Enga beats. I repeat do not, as I said, I’m not going to allow this to happen in Kenya,” Magix asserted.

Harmonize's management, however, denied the violation alleging someone from Kenya was trying to claim rights to their song.

“We have received a statement from Youtube that someone from Kenya, has e-mailed to claim ownership of our favorite song Uno. We take this opportunity to ask our fans to be patient, while this matter is handled. In addition, we believe our song will be back soon," part of the statement from the star’s manager read.

The beats in question were used in Magix’s hit Dundaing, which featured the likes of King Kaka.

The Uno song had become a fan's favourite as the singer held an East African tour for the audio promotion.
