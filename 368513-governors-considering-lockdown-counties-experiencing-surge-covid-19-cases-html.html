The Council of Governors (COG) has threatened to push for lockdown in counties that are experiencing an increase in the number of infections.

Of the total 11, 252 cases of COVID-19 confirmed in Kenya since March, Nairobi continues to lead with 6,245 followed by Mombasa (1,768), Kiambu (602), Busia (502) and Kajiado (526).

Since President Uhuru Kenyatta ended the cessation of movement on July 6, some 3,588 new infections have been reported within 10 days.

The new cases were announced in Nairobi (2,290), Kiambu (264), Machakos (247), Kajiado (177) and Mombasa (150).

Others were Busia (125), Nakuru (71), Migori (70) and Uasin Gishu (36).

The coronavirus pandemic is real and is here with us. Community infections are surging daily and counties near the border and the capital city Nairobi where the first case was recorded are bearing the brunt.

COG chairman Wycliffe Oparanya said county bosses were taking all the necessary measures to curb the spread of the highly contagious disease.

"County govts' are on high alert to stem inter-county spread of COVID-19. If the situation gets dire, individual counties will have no choice but in consultation with the president, seek to lock down the affected county to contain the spread and protect lives," Oparanya said in a statement.

While opening the country, President Uhuru Kenyatta said it was upon everyone to take care of themselves.

You must become your brother's keeper and while the government will do what is required, you, my fellow citizens must also do what is expected of you," the head of state said.

He, however, warned the country would revert to lockdown if the increasing cases posed a challenge to the health infrastructure.

"Should the situation deteriorate and pose a challenge to our health infrastructure, we will have to revert back to lockdown," he added.
