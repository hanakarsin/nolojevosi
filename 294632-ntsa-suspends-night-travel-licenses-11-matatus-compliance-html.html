NTSA suspends night travel licenses of 11 matatus over non-compliance

July 17, 2024
Post

- Mbukinya Sacco bus plying Nairobi-Western Kenya route lost night travel license alongside four other Passenger Service Vehicles

- Six vehicles lost road service licenses and will be away from the roads for 30 days

- National Transport and Safety Authority boss Francis Meja said night accidents were on the rise

- Inspector General of Police Joseph Boinnet ordered for confiscation of drivers found driving while drunk

- Seven drivers have lost their licenses already for drunk driving

National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has withdrawn licenses of 11 matatus after they were found not to have complied with fundamental regulations of service.

Out of the 11 Passenger Service Vehicles (PSVs), five had their night travel licenses suspended for 30 days over non-compliance while six others lost road service licenses for traffic violations

In a statement to the press seen by TUKO.co.ke, NTSA stated operators of the 11 vehicles failed to comply with section 7 (c) and 11 (1) of NTSA regulations 2014 and got maximum penalty of 30 days suspension from operation.

Francis Meja, NTSA Director General raised concern many accidents involving PSVs happen during night travel.

"The authority is keen on inspection in a bid to reduce the risk by ensuring vehicles were in total good condition," Meja said.

Mbukinya Sacco car number KBZ 691E, Ongata Rongai Sacco vehicle KBV 520L, Kenya Poa Sacco car number KCJ 128A, Unified Sacco vehicle registration number KBT 227D and Nazigi Sacco's KBZ 205X all lost night operation licenses.

The following lost road service licenses, Marimba Sacco's KCG 553P, KCF 883F of Prestige Sacco, Nawasuko Sacco's KCD 358Q, Latema Sacco car number KBS 335D, Kibera Bureti Sacco's KCR 051D and Lucky Baba Dogo Sacco's KBZ 390B.

This comes after Inspector General of Police Joseph Boinnet directed traffic officers to impound licenses of motorists found driving while drunk saying the trend had contributed to numerous accidents.

Already the courts have withdrawn driving licenses of seven motorists who were arrested for drunk driving.

Six of the motorists were suspended for one year with one being banned against driving on Kenyans Kenyan roads for two years.
