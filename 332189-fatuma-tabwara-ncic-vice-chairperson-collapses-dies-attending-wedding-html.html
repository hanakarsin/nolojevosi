Fatuma Tabwara: Newly appointed NCIC vice chair collapses, dies at wedding in Kwale

June 07, 2024
Post

- Tabwara had attended a wedding of her relative in Diani area in Kwale county

- The former Governor Mvurya's advisor collapsed and was rushed to the hospital

- Doctors confirmed she passed on upon arrival at the Diani Cottage Hospital

The recently appointed National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC) vice chairperson Fatuma Tabwara is dead.

Fatuma collapsed and died in Diani, Kwale county, where she was reportedly attending a wedding ceremony of her niece.

She was rushed to Diani Cottage Hospital but efforts to resuscitate her proved futile after she took her last breath upon arrival.

Further unconfirmed reports indicated Fatuma suffered a severe heart attack.

Her body has since been taken to her home awaiting burial arrangement in line with Islamic doctrines.

The former political and policy advisor to Governor Salim Mvurya was sworn into office in November 2019 after successful and rigorous vetting process.

The deceased holds a master's degree in Education Planning and Administration and a bachelor’s degree in Education.
