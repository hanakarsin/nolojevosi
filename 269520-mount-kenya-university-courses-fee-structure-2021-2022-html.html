Mount Kenya University is a registered and fully recognized broad-based Kenyan-based university. The institution is known for its dynamic, inclusive, and thorough academic and vocational training system. In addition, MKU is keen on progressive human resource development. The university's main campus is based in Thika town, off Nairobi City, the capital city of Kenya. The institution has tapped into massive industrialization currently taking place in the city. Mount Kenya university courses are unmatched and respectable locally and internationally.

MKU is a chartered university offering extensive academic programs to many learners now enrolled in the institution. The institution is ISO 9001:2008 certified and is an active member of the Association of Common Wealth Universities and East African Inter-University Council.

Despite Mount Kenya University being private, its tuition fees are priced competitively compared to how much public universities charge self-sponsored students. Here is a breakdown of the MKU fee structure:

PHDs

You will pay Ksh. 200,000 yearly for a PhD. In any course under the School of Pure and Applied Sciences, you will pay Ksh. 150,000 per year. A Doctor of Philosophy PhD. It will cost you Ksh. 250,000 per year.

Masters

A Masters degree will cost you Ksh. 59,550 per semester. A Master of Health-related course will cost you Ksh. 70,000 to Ksh. 79,550. A clinical Medicine Master's will cost you Ksh. 104,550 per semester.

Degree

A degree in health-related courses will cost you Ksh. 54,550 to Ksh. 85,550. For other Schools, this figure is as low as Ksh. 45,550 to Ksh. 59,550 per semester. A Bachelor of medicine and surgery will cost you Ksh. 129,550 per semester.
