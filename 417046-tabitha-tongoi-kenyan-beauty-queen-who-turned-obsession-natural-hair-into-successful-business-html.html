Kenyan beauty queen, Tabitha Tongoi, probably needs no introduction. She is one of the most influential YouTubers and beauty content vloggers in Kenya today.

Tongoi, a social media influencer obsessed with natural hair and beauty, started her popular YouTube channel Craving Yellow with a borrowed camera in 2015. She was 24.

She has over the years managed to turn her obsession with natural hair into a flourishing business with a focus on matters close to women's hearts.

What started as a hobby earning nothing has evolved into a revenue stream with a consistent online presence across several online platforms.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, the youthful entrepreneur opened up on how she started her business which currently has over 200,000 followers spread across YouTube (69,700), Instagram (107,000), Facebook (26,000) and Twitter (3,379).

Craving Yellow started as an idea in school

Tongoi said Craving Yellow was an idea that started out of loneliness when she was a student studying political science at Williams College, Massachusetts, United States of America.

"I was really looking to connect with my fellow African women online about matters that affected us - at the time I was a student living abroad, and I was very lonely. Hair has always been that icebreaker amongst women in the diaspora.""Craving Yellow YouTube channel focuses mainly on haircare - but I love to share about matters close to a woman’s heart - be it love, career, family, relationships and even business," she said.

Tongoi began by offering hair care tips and tricks, particularly caring for one’s natural hair.

The 30-year-old has since moved into lifestyle vlogs and fashion content. She also loves to host interviews with fellow influencers on life and career.

Her business which is her side hustle, she said, earns her enough money to enable her to pay bills.

Cost of creating YouTube content

But how much does it cost to create content for YouTube?

Tongoi said it all boils down to patience and creativity.

"I always say you don’t need to start with much. Just what you have. I started off with a borrowed camera, and I’m here today," she told TUKO.co.ke.

She admits there are several challenges in the industry that she noted had no role models when she joined. Nevertheless, the digital space, she stated, has evolved in recent years.

"Digital media is a very new space, so we had no one to show us the way. We had to learn and make mistakes as we went along. I’m glad that the new generation of social media influencers have an older generation to look up to."

Amid the COVID-19 pandemic that has ravaged the world, Tongoi said her online platforms, especially YouTube, offered her space to connect with more people considering that face-to-face interactions have been limited.

She took on a new study - nutrition and is an active member within nutrition circles.

"Through my offline career, I have also been able to create paid opportunities for many upcoming influencers - as most brands have had to reach their consumer online because of COVID-19 restrictions. So enjoy the journey, don’t compare yourself to others, and trust the process!"
