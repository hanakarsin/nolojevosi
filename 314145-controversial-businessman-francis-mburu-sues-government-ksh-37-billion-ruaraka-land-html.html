Controversial businessman Francis Mburu sues government for KSh 36 billion over Ruaraka land

June 13, 2024
Post

Businessman Francis Mburu on Thursday, August 29, sued the government seeking more than KSh 36 billion in compensation for the disputed Ruaraka land.

He sued the attorney general, National Land Commission and the Nairobi county government for compensation of the 39.5 acres prime land.

He said the market value for the land is KSh 25 billion but is demanded an additional KSh 13.6 billion in compensation for loss of rent plus accrued interests.

According to the businessman, Ruaraka High and Drive In Primary schools have not paid rent for 34 years which has since accrued to KSh 5.9 billion.

Continental Credit Finance Limited is the custodian of the property.

The land is occupied by the two schools and the GSU camp next to the Kenya School of Monetary Studies.

A section of the land was acquired for expansion of the Outering road.

The government compulsorily acquired 13.5 acres of the land for the two schools in 2017 at a cost of KSh 3.2 billion and paid KSh 1.2 billion only.

Following partial payment of the compensation, Mburu said various accusations were leveled against him.

The initial land was 96 acres and he claimed he acquired it in 1981 from Joreth Limited.

Mburu entered into an agreement with the Kenya Posts and Telecommunications Corporation to build 1, 200 maisonettes on the whole land. CCFL was to finance the project to the tune of KSh 160 million.

Out of the projected houses, only 196 units were completed on an un-surveyed plot measuring 7.5 acres.

The units house GSU officers.

The land was further subdivided and 35 acres hived off which are occupied by the GSU and KURA.
