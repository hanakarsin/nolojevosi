Anxiety as strange gullies emerge in Naivasha

March 28, 2024
Post

Residents of Moi Ndabi, Naivasha sub county are living in fear of a possible landslide after fault lines emerged in the area cutting off transport and destroying property.

The deep cracks began to show about two weeks ago ahead of the heavy rainfall which is currently being experienced across the country.

One resident Kiroket Membarin, the rains further weakened gullies which have widens each passing day despite experts visiting the area.

“Geologists from various institutes including those from University of Nairobi visited the village but their findings were never made public and now the same problem has returned,” he said.

Though there is no official communication from the Geologist Society of Kenya, the trend is reminiscent of a similar one witnessed when section of Narok-Nairobi highway sank in 2018.

In 2018, bridge between Suswa and Duka Moja on Narok-Nairobi road swept away by the floods amid a heavy downpour.

According to the Met department, the devastating and thunderstorms are expected in Western, Rift Valley, Central highlands and Nairobi in the last week of April 2019 and may spill over into new month.
