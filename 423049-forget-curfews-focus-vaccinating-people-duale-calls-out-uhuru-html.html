Forget about Curfews and Focus on Vaccinating People, Duale Tells Uhuru

August 04, 2024

  • The extension of the nationwide 10pm to 4am curfew is aimed at curbing the spread of the dreaded COVID-19
  • Social gatherings like weddings, celebrations of marriage or traditional unions ceremonies and funerals will be attended by not more than 100 people

Garissa Township MP Aden Duale has hit out at President Uhuru Kenyatta following the extension of the nationwide 10pm to 4am curfew.

On Wednesday, August 18, Uhuru said the curfew would run for another 60 days to curb the spread of COVID-19.

Acquire more vaccines

Reacting to the announcement, Duale told off Uhuru over the extension of the curfew period, urging him to acquire more vaccines for the Kenyan populace.

''As a country, and I have said it on the floor of the House, we need to forget about these extensions of curfews and all these and start vaccinating the people. We need to have a severe programme of vaccination of our people,'' Duale said during a talk show on Spice FM.

The immediate former majority leader of the National Assembly said there is a need to vaccinate up to 30 million Kenyans, mainly in the old age bracket.

''The best thing is that the resources to ensure all these have been provided for. So these are some of the things that amaze some of us because, in the last budget, we passed about KSh 34 billion for purchasing vaccines because we have an issue in terms of where to buy, most of the developed countries don't want to sell, they want to give priority,'' he explained.

Duale also accused the country's top leadership of being at the forefront in violating COVID-19 regulations.

''It is very shameful that a government can contradict itself. I watched with disbelief the president impose a ban on public gatherings, including political rallies, because I knew the Orange. At the same time, Deputy President William Ruto will host a delegation ahis Karen residence,'' said Duale.

Uhuru banned public gatherings

In a statement on Wednesday, August 18, the head of state also banned public gatherings and meetings, including political rallies and campaign meetings for impending by-elections for another 60 days.

"All public gatherings and political rallies are extended for another 60 days to tame the infection rate of COVID-19 which is ravaging the country," read the statement.

At the same time, the president relaxed the COVID-19 restriction in the zoned counties, citing Kisumu, Migori, Homa Bay, Siaya, Vihiga, Busia, Bungoma and Trans Nzoia, among other counties.

Social gatherings like weddings, celebrations of marriage or traditional unions ceremonies and funerals will be attended by not more than 100 people.

As of Wednesday, August 18, the Ministry of Health had vaccinated 2.1 million Kenyans.

The country has received 2.7 million vaccines, with the second dose uptake standing at 54.6%.
