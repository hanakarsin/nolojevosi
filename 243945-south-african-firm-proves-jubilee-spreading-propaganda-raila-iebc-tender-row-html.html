South African firm 'proves' Jubilee spreading propaganda about Raila in IEBC tender row

March 22, 2024
Post

- NASA claims Jubilee influenced the awarding of the KSh 2.5B IEBC tender deal to a Dubai based firm with close links to the Kenyatta family

- This was done in order to print extra ballot papers to rig the August 8 General Election

- Jubilee then claimed Raila was only angry because the firm he wanted to win the tender did not win, therefore losing a KSh 1 billion kickback he'd been promised

- Now, the South African firm linked to Raila has spoken on the matter, claiming Jubilee is merely spreading outrageous lies and propaganda

- The firm also said they would never pay KSh 1 billion kickback for a KSh 2.5 billion business deal

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission ballot paper tender row continued to unravel after the National Super Alliance and the Jubilee Party took on each other for allegedly attempting to influence who got the tender to print the papers.

It all started when NASA revealed that the printing firm that won the tender, Al Ghurair, a Dubai based company, is close with the Kenyatta family, more so with President Uhuru’s elder brother, Muhoho Kenyatta.

Days later, Jubilee sensationally claimed that NASA, led by their presidential candidate Raila Odinga, found fault with the tender award because the company he was championing to win the tender lost.

According to previous TUKO.co.ke reports, Raila Odinga, acting on behalf of South African company, Paarl Media, was to help them get them the KSh 2.5 billion tender for a kick back.

Apparently, the printing firm promised Raila KSh 1 billion for his campaigns after a number of meetings in Kenya and South Africa.

On Saturday, June 17, speaking to the Standard, Paarl Media’s business development manager, Ben Sachs, denied meeting Raila Odinga, and termed the accusations as pure lies.
