On Friday, June 25, President Uhuru Kenyatta went down on his knees to pray at Our Lady of Protection Shrine, Museve in Kitui county.

The head of state had been invited to the Marian Shrine's official opening and dedication ceremony.

According to a statement issued by State House, Nairobi, the shrine was developed by the Catholic Diocese of Kitui to serve as a privileged place of prayer, retreat and recollections.

"President Uhuru Kenyatta has urged Kenyans to continue praying for peace and national unity, saying that is the route to achieving the country’s development goals. He challenged politicians to emulate religious leaders by working together for the common good of all Kenyans," part of the statement read.

Uhuru asked Kenyans to love each other to build the nation for the benefit of all Kenyans."

"Let’s love each other as children of God regardless of tribe, colour or religion."

The 26-acre Marian Shrine was consecrated by Archbishop Anthony Muheria of the Nyeri Catholic Diocese assisted by Bishop Joseph Mwongela of Kitui.

Uhuru was accompanied by several national and Ukambani leaders. They included former vice president Kalonzo Musyoka, Kitui governor Charity Ngilu, Defence Cabinet Secretary Monica Juma and Central Bank of Kenya Governor Patrick Njoroge.

National Prayer Breakfast

In May 2021, Uhuru was also joined by other leaders including his embattled deputy, William Ruto to pray for the country during the National Prayer Breakfast that was held at Parliament Buildings.

The session began with a prayer from Senate Speaker Ken Lusaka followed by a prayer for national leadership by Senator Rose Nyamunga.

The recently appointed Chief Justice Martha Koome led a prayer for families and the children of Kenya.

During the event, one Peter Waiyaki made the keynote address themed 'a hopeful future'.

In his address, Waiyaki suggested a few things that Kenyans can do without background to guarantee the country's hope it is looking at.

"Internalise and insist on integrity. Whoever we are, however low or high. Whatever our situation and situation in life, we need to commit to integrity. Integrity is doing what you said you would do when you said you would do it and how you said you would do it," Waiyaki said.

He described integrity as the action of keeping your word, your promise even when it hurts you to do so.

"It is choosing when faced with the choices the hard-right than the easy wrong," he told guests.
