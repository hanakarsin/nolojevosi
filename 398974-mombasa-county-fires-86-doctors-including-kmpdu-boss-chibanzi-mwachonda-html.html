Mombasa county fires 86 doctors including KMPDU boss Chibanzi Mwachonda

June 04, 2024
Post

- Mwachonda has been leading doctors in protesting against poor remuneration by the government

- He has been championing for enhancement medics' salaries, risk allowances and medical cover among other grievances

- Mombasa county government has axed him alongside 85 others for refusing to return to work since December 21

Mombasa county government has dismissed 86 doctors who have refused to return to work since they commenced their industrial action in December 2020.

The 86 medics included Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentist Union (KMPDU) boss Chibanzi Mwachonda and other senior specialists.

The medics downed their tools of work on December 21 in a bid to push the government to enhance their salaries, risk allowances and medical cover among other grievances.

They negotiated with the officials from the ministry of health and agreed on a return to work formula but governors through their chairman Wycliff Oparanya rejected the deal.

Oparanya said governors were not consulted by the national government before inking the deal. He further argued counties did not no money to cater for what had been agreed upon.

On December 23, TUKO.co.ke reported on a directive issued by Oparanya to all governors asking them to fire all doctors who had refused to return to work.

The Council of Governors' chair who doubles up as the Kakamega governor said the decision was arrived at after a lengthy discussion with the national government and other stakeholders in the health docket.

He noted the doctors' strike was illegal since the court had suspended it to allow for further negotiations.

"We note with utmost concern that unions have failed to adhere to the court orders by failing to call off the ongoing strike. This has negatively affected service delivery in county health facilities," read the statement in part.

Oparanya asked governors to stop salaries for striking health workers and commence the process of recruiting new staff with immediate effect.

"Effect stoppage of salaries where employees have not rendered their services. This is in accordance with section 79(6) and 80(1)(b) of the Labour Relations act.
